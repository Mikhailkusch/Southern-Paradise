Headline · Facebook views

Up 94% month-on-month. Our biggest month on Facebook to date.

Chisomo's Sawing Call, the leopard-vocalisation reel released in March, kept building through April, reaching 4.7M views and pulling 11,400 net new Facebook followers behind it. Instagram held steady at our off-season median, adding 859 followers and posting 593.9K views.

The bigger story sits inside the Facebook numbers. With Americans now making up 15.1% of the audience, our US following alone is roughly 18,400 people, six times the size of the entire page when we first took it over.

A site visit to Zambia is planned for July; performing at this level during off-season is the strongest possible signal for what's ahead.

XIV Strategic Outlook

What's working, and what's next.

What's working

Character-driven storytelling continues to break through.

Chisomo's Sawing Call, released last month and still climbing, is the clearest evidence yet that audiences want named animals, recognisable behaviour, and the rhythm of a single life unfolding. Facebook is flying. Instagram holds median during off-season, the strongest possible signal for what happens once we're back on the ground.

What's next

Lean into the cast. Add a UGC layer.

We'll keep leaning into the cast, Chisomo, Taonga, and the broader pride. With a Zambia site visit planned for July, on-the-ground material will return to the pipeline. Alongside that, we're shaping a deliberate UGC layer, short-form, raw-feel content that can outperform polished work, and is performing strongly on other accounts across socials in 2026. With our polished work already driving the numbers, bringing this additional layer in next season will add real personality to our social presence.

The trajectory continues.
The next leg is on-site.
